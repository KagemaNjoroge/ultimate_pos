Inventory Fields Guide

Quantity
Total physical units in stock. This is your actual inventory count.
Alert Quantity
When stock reaches this level, you'll get low stock alerts. Set this higher than your minimum stock level for early warnings.
Reserved Quantity
Items already allocated for pending orders. These are not available for new sales.
Available Quantity
Calculated as: Total Quantity - Reserved Quantity. This is what's actually available for sale.
Minimum Stock Level
The reorder point. When stock hits this level, it's time to place new orders with suppliers.
Maximum Stock Level
The maximum quantity to maintain. Helps prevent overstocking and capital tie-up.

Cost Price
The price you paid to acquire this item. Used for calculating profits and inventory valuation.
Selling Price
The price customers pay. Should be higher than cost price to ensure profitability.
Tip: Maintain a healthy margin between cost and selling price to cover operational expenses and generate profit.

Shelf/Bin Location
Specific location within the warehouse (e.g., "A1-B2", "Shelf 3", "Bin 15"). Helps staff locate items quickly.
Warehouse
The storage facility or warehouse name. Useful for businesses with multiple locations.

Batch Number
Manufacturer's batch/lot number. Groups products made together for quality control and recalls.
Serial Number
Unique identifier for individual items. Essential for warranties and asset tracking.
Supplier SKU
The supplier's product code. Helpful when reordering or communicating with suppliers.

Active
Item is available for sale. Inactive items won't appear in sales interfaces.
Damaged
Item is damaged and cannot be sold. Excluded from available stock calculations.
Expired
Item has passed its expiry date. Critical for perishables, medicines, and regulated products.
